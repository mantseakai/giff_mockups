Workflow Purpose

Move GIFF from manual records to controlled digital operations

This mockup is designed to present the end-to-end digitization sequence for GIFF, showing how paper files, spreadsheets, and legacy registers can be assessed, standardized, digitized, cleaned, migrated, tested, and operationalized with strong controls.

Implementation Principles

How the rollout stays low-risk

Phase the migration: start with membership, then dues, then accounts, then HR.
Keep auditability: maintain logs, approvals, and source-file backups.
Validate before go-live: reconcile totals, sample records, and access rules.
Workflow Diagram

GIFF Digitization Technical Workflow

Redesigned from the technical workflow document into a presentation-ready mockup that can be opened directly in a browser during proposal or stakeholder sessions.

1

Assess

Review registers, forms, spreadsheets, and filing systems. Identify owners, pain points, duplicates, gaps, and departmental scope.

2

Define

Create digital fields, codes, naming standards, and validation rules for IDs, dates, balances, and staff records.

3

Capture

Scan paper files, run OCR where practical, and use manual entry for structured records that require accuracy and review.

4

Clean

Deduplicate, normalize formatting, validate against source documents, and flag suspicious or incomplete records.

5

Migrate to Digital Platform

Import cleansed data into one secure system in controlled batches, with status tracking, exception handling, and approval checkpoints.

Membership Dues & Fees Accounts HR
6-8

Validate, Train, Go-Live, Support

Verify migrated records, test reporting and approvals, train GIFF teams on new workflows, then deploy in phases with support, backups, and audit controls.

6. Testing & Reconciliation Confirm record accuracy, sample-check totals, and validate reporting, billing, search, and access control functions.
7. Training & Change Train users on roles, approvals, data entry, reporting, and supervised adoption through pilot use.
8. Go-Live & Support Deploy in phases, monitor usage, fix issues early, preserve audit trails, and archive manual records securely.
Recommended rule: digitize in phases, not all at once. Start with membership, then dues and fees, then accounts, then HR.
Migration Sequence
Priority Module Why First
Priority 1 Membership Management Stable records and identity structure needed by other modules.
Priority 2 Dues & Fees Administration Builds directly on verified member records and billing cycles.
Priority 3 Accounts Requires reconciled member and fee transactions for clean opening balances.
Priority 4 Human Resource Management Can follow once controls, users, and document workflows are stable.
